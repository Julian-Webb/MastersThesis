\section{Propensity Evaluation}\label{sec:propensity-evaluation}

\subsection{Statistical Results} \label{subsec:statistical-results}
From the twelve included patients, two models per patient, and 15 features, there were a total of 360 patient-model-feature combinations.
\Cref{fig:qualifications_flow_chart} shows where combinations were excluded based on the criteria from \cref{sec:propensity-eval}.
Only the three combinations (\SI{0.83}{\percent}) shown in \Cref{tab:model-feature-qualifications} met all criteria.
For the remaining 357 combinations (\SI{99.27}{\percent}), the assumptions did not hold, implying that either the seizures or \glspl{fp} were not phase locked to the features, the model's performance was too poor, or the phase distributions of seizures and \glspl{fp} were not similar enough.

\subsection{Descriptive Results} \label{subsec:descriptive-results}
% todo decide whether to show U17 first and move the detailed caption accordingly, as well as adjust figure widths
Even though only very few patient-model-feature combinations met the formal criteria, there were various observable effects that weren't captured by the statistical tests.
These are examined in this section.

\begin{figure}[p]
    \centering

    \begin{subfigure}[t]{\textwidth}
        \centering
        \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{phase_histograms/U01/seizures}
        \caption{Seizures}
        \label{fig:U01-seizure-phases}
    \end{subfigure}

    \medskip

    \begin{subfigure}[t]{\textwidth}
        \centering
        \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{phase_histograms/U01/CNN FPs}
        \caption{CNN FPs}
        \label{fig:U01-CNN-FP-phases}
    \end{subfigure}

    \caption[Phase Histograms per Feature of Patient U01 for seizures and CNN FPs]{Phase Histograms per Feature and for seizures and \gls{cnn} \glspl{fp} of \textbf{Patient U01}.}
    \label{fig:U01-phase-histograms}
\end{figure}

For patient U01, there was a significant phase preference for seizures (\cref{fig:U01-seizure-phases}), \gls{cnn} \glspl{fp} (\cref{fig:U01-CNN-FP-phases}), and ensemble \glspl{fp} (\cref{fig:appendix-phase-histogram-U01-ensemble FPs}), which is also clearly visible in the figures.
However, between seizures and \glspl{fp}, there was usually a phase delay, i.e., \glspl{fp} tended to occur later than seizures.
This delay is also visible when inspecting the filtered signal with seizures and \glspl{fp} (\cref{fig:U01-filtered-var-P}): the \glspl{fp} tended to occur during portions with many seizures, but also frequently thereafter, more so than before, which is consistent with the detected shift of phase preference.
This strong phase preference, albeit being shifted, was only present for patient U01.

\begin{figure}[hbtp]
    \centering
    \includegraphics{src/figures/U01_filtered_var_P.pdf}
    \caption{The Original and Filtered Signal of Feature Var. (P) of Patient U01, including the occurrences of seizures and FPs}
    \label{fig:U01-filtered-var-P}
\end{figure}

Other patients usually did not exhibit a significant phase preference of events (seizures or \glspl{fp}), or only did so for few combinations of features and event types.
For example, for patient C03, seizures were only significantly phase locked to some or no features (\cref{fig:C03-seizure-phases}), and ensemble \glspl{fp} (\cref{fig:C03-ensemble-FP-phases}) and \gls{cnn} \glspl{fp} were never significantly phase locked to features.
Accordingly, there were few or no combinations of event types and features that exhibited a strong phase preference across patients, except for U01.
For further examination, all phase histograms are shown in \cref{ch:phase-histograms} and all filtered feature plots with event occurrences in \cref{ch:filtered-feature-plots}.
