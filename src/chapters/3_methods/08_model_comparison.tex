\section{Model Comparison}\label{sec:model-comparison}
For a statistical comparison of the performance of the different models across patients, each patient was treated as a paired observation, and it is evaluated whether significant outperformance is achieved by one model over the other.

Because both models are tested on the same set of patients, their performance metrics (e.g., ROC AUC or \gls{eb} score) are not independent but paired.
Additionally, a normal distribution of performance metric across patients is not guaranteed, and the sample size is limited (n~=~12 patients).
Therefore, a non-parametric paired test, namely the Wilcoxon signed-rank test, is used.
Unlike parametric alternatives (e.g., paired t-test), greater robustness to outliers and skewed distributions is offered by this test, making it a safer and more reliable choice in this setting.

By applying this test separately to the relative \gls{tifw}, \gls{eb} sensitivity, \gls{eb} score, and \gls{roc auc}, it is assessed whether the median difference in performance between the two models deviates significantly from zero, thereby providing evidence for systematic superiority of one model over the other.
