\section{Propensity Evaluation}\label{sec:propensity-evaluation}

\subsection{Statistical Results} \label{subsec:statistical-results}
From the twelve included patients, two models per patient, and 15 features, there were a total of 360 patient-model-feature combinations.
\Cref{fig:qualifications_flow_chart} shows where combinations were excluded based on the criteria from \cref{sec:propensity-eval}.
Only the three combinations (\SI{0.83}{\percent}) shown in \Cref{tab:model-feature-qualifications} met all criteria.
For the remaining 357 combinations (\SI{99.27}{\percent}), the assumptions did not hold, implying that either the seizures or \glspl{fp} were not phase locked to the features, the model's performance was too poor, or the phase distributions of seizures and \glspl{fp} were not similar enough.

\subsection{Descriptive Results} \label{subsec:descriptive-results}
Even though only very few patient-model-feature combinations met the criteria, we visually observed an effect for patient U01.
His seizures had a clear preferred phase (\cref{fig:U01-seizure-phases}).
Likewise, his \glspl{fp} had a clear preferred phase for both the \gls{cnn} (\cref{fig:U01-CNN-FP-phases}) and ensemble (\cref{fig:U01-ensemble-FP-phases}).
However, between seizures and \glspl{fp}, there was a phase delay, i.e., \glspl{fp} tended to occur somewhat later than seizures.
This could also be seen when we inspected the filtered signal with seizures and \glspl{fp} in \cref{fig:U01_filtered_var_P}: the \glspl{fp} tended to occur during portions with many seizures, but also frequently thereafter, rather than before.
This explained the detected shifted phase preference.
This effect was only pronounced for patient U01.

For other patients, this was not the case.
Patient C03 acted as an example for cases where seizures were only phase locked to some or no features (\cref{fig:C03-seizure-phases}) and \glspl{fp} were not phase locked to features (\cref{fig:C03-CNN-FP-phases}, \cref{fig:C03-ensemble-FP-phases}).
This was more typical of the phase locking observed across patients, except for U01.
